Why it exists

Shopping sites are built to go fast.

Timers, urgency copy, and one-click checkout are there to shorten the gap between impulse and order. That can work against you when you’re tired or distracted.

Dilly doesn’t block purchases. It adds a short pause at the moment you try to add to cart, so you can choose with a clear head.

Take a pause.

You stay in control: wait out the timer and add the item, or tap “not today.” Optional stats are just for you — if you enable them, they stay in your browser.

How it works

Dilly runs only when you try to add something to your cart (or a similar checkout step).

01 — Browse

Shop as usual

Browse normally. Dilly stays out of the way until you try to add to cart or buy.

02 — Pause

Short pause

A small overlay appears with a timer you set (often starting around 10 seconds; you can choose 30, 60, or more in settings).

03 — Decide

You choose

When the timer ends, continue to checkout or step away. Optional stats stay only on your computer.

FAQ

Common questions

Quick answers about how Dilly fits into your shopping.

Does Dilly stop me from buying things?

No. After the pause ends, you can add to bag or close the overlay. Dilly adds a moment to decide — it doesn’t block checkout.

Do I need an account?

No sign-up. When Dilly is on the Chrome Web Store, you’ll install it like any extension and use it locally. Optional stats stay on your device.

Is my browsing data sent anywhere?

Dilly is built for privacy-first use: nothing is synced to our servers for accounts you don’t have. Behaviour stays in your browser per Chrome’s extension model.

When does the pause appear?

When you try to add something to your cart (or a similar step on supported sites). You can adjust thresholds and pause length in settings.

Is Dilly free?

Yes — free to install. No in-app subscriptions pitched here.

Which sites does it work on?

Dilly is built for shopping sites where you add items to a cart or bag. It won’t run on every URL — it activates on supported checkout-style flows. If something doesn’t trigger yet, settings and updates over time can improve coverage.

Does it work on mobile?

Dilly is a Chrome desktop extension. It doesn’t run in mobile Safari or Chrome on phones the same way. Use it in Chrome on your computer.

Can I pause or turn Dilly off?

Yes. You can adjust when pauses happen, how long they last, and use your browser’s extension controls to disable Dilly anytime — you stay in charge.
